Coral Gables Real Estate

Mediterranean elegance and tree-lined luxury — one of Miami's most established, family-oriented enclaves.

Coral Gables — 'The City Beautiful' — is one of Miami's most established luxury markets: Mediterranean-revival estates, banyan-shaded boulevards, and a civic character that feels distinct from the rest of the city.

Why invest in Coral Gables?

The Gables is a blue-chip, single-family market. Values have shown remarkable resilience — the median sale price rose about 22.7% year over year to roughly $1.65 million, and luxury homes trade around $1,245 per square foot. For Q1 2026, the single-family luxury threshold (top 5% of sales) reached $4.1 million and the ultra-luxury threshold (top 1%) hit $13.6 million, both up sharply from 2025.

The market is increasingly selective. Today's buyers reward clarity: hurricane-resilient homes with impact glass and recent roof certifications, and turnkey, move-in-ready properties that clear the market noticeably faster than project homes.

What is it like to live in Coral Gables?

Coral Gables offers A-rated public and private schools, walkable streets shaded by banyan trees, the shops and dining of Miracle Mile, and landmarks like the Biltmore Hotel and Venetian Pool. It's the natural fit for families and buyers who want space, privacy, and long-term stability over high-rise density.

Questions, answered

Coral Gables real estate FAQ

What makes Coral Gables a premier luxury market?

Coral Gables pairs historic Mediterranean architecture, top-rated schools, and tree-lined walkability with consistently resilient values — the median sale price rose about 22.7% year over year to roughly $1.65 million.

What is the average home price in Coral Gables?

Recent data puts the median sale price near $1.65 million, with luxury single-family homes trading around $1,245 per square foot; the top-5% luxury threshold is about $4.1 million.

What are buyers prioritizing in Coral Gables right now?

Hurricane resilience (impact glass, recent roof certifications) and turnkey, move-in-ready condition — those homes are selling faster than properties needing work.

Is Coral Gables good for families?

Yes — A-rated schools, low-density streets, parks, and a strong civic identity make it one of Miami's most family-oriented luxury communities.
