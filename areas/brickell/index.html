Brickell Real Estate

Miami's financial district — walkable skyline living with some of the strongest luxury-condo fundamentals in the U.S.

Brickell is Miami's financial core and its most dynamic urban-condo market — a dense, walkable grid of glass towers, waterfront residences, restaurants, and banking headquarters just south of the Miami River.

Why invest in Brickell?

Brickell has been one of the strongest-performing luxury condo markets in the country, powered by limited land supply, steady corporate relocations, and Florida's lack of a state income tax. In Q1 2026 the neighborhood entered its most buyer-friendly stretch since 2019 — roughly 17 months of supply and about 113 days on market — which gives buyers real negotiating leverage while the top end keeps climbing. Sales of $1M+ residences rose about 21% year over year.

For investors, the rental math is a big part of the story. Median rents run roughly $4,050–$4,200 a month, which on a median-priced condo translates to a gross yield in the 6–7% range — unusually strong for a major U.S. urban core.

What is it like to live in Brickell?

Brickell rewards a lock-and-leave, walk-everywhere lifestyle. Residents are minutes from Brickell City Centre, Mary Brickell Village, the Underline linear park, and a short drive or Metromover ride to Downtown, the Design District, and the airport. It suits professionals, international buyers, and investors who want turnkey, amenity-rich living with genuine street life.

Questions, answered

Brickell real estate FAQ

Is Brickell a good place to invest in real estate?

Brickell combines limited land, strong rental demand, and no Florida state income tax, and has posted some of the best luxury-condo performance in the U.S. As of Q1 2026 it also favors buyers, with elevated inventory creating room to negotiate. As with any purchase, returns depend on the specific building, unit, and timing — I'm happy to walk you through current numbers.

What is the average condo price in Brickell?

Recent closings put the median Brickell condo around $620,000–$660,000. Mid-tier buildings trade roughly $500–$700 per square foot, while ultra-luxury and branded waterfront residences reach $1,800–$2,600 per square foot.

What kind of rental returns does Brickell offer?

Median rents are approximately $4,050–$4,200 per month, which on a median-priced condo works out to a gross yield in the 6–7% range as of 2026. Actual yields vary by building, financing, and rental strategy.

Who is Brickell best suited for?

Professionals and international buyers who want walkable, amenity-rich, lock-and-leave living, and investors seeking strong urban rental fundamentals.
