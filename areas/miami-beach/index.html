Miami Beach Real Estate

Oceanfront living and island glamour — from South of Fifth to Bal Harbour, home to Miami's trophy properties.

Miami Beach is the barrier-island icon: oceanfront towers, walkable South Beach, and the trophy enclaves of South of Fifth, Surfside, and Bal Harbour that define the very top of the Miami market.

Why invest in Miami Beach?

Miami Beach sits at the apex of the market and keeps setting records. In early 2026 the average list price reached about $2.69 million (roughly $1,063 per square foot), and the luxury-condo tier recorded a median sale price of $8.3 million in mid-2025 — up about 10% year over year, the highest quarterly median on record. Sales of $5M+ properties surged around 25% year over year.

This is a scarcity-driven, trophy market. Oceanfront in South of Fifth, Surfside, and Bal Harbour continues to see firm-to-record pricing, and cash buyers dominate — about 44% of Miami-Dade closings were all-cash in early 2026, a signal of the wealth concentrated here.

What is it like to live in Miami Beach?

Life on the Beach ranges from the energy of South Beach to the quiet, guard-gated calm of the islands and Bal Harbour's luxury retail. Buyers get direct beach access, world-class dining and hotels, and a walkable, resort lifestyle — with the premium that trophy oceanfront commands.

Questions, answered

Miami Beach real estate FAQ

Is Miami Beach still a strong luxury market?

Yes — it sits at the top of the market and keeps setting records, with the luxury-condo median reaching $8.3 million in mid-2025 and $5M+ sales up about 25% year over year.

What is the average price in Miami Beach?

The average list price was around $2.69 million in early 2026 (about $1,063 per square foot), with trophy oceanfront reaching far higher.

Which Miami Beach areas are most exclusive?

South of Fifth, Bal Harbour, Surfside, and the guard-gated islands command the highest prices and the strongest trophy demand.

Do buyers pay cash in Miami Beach?

Frequently — about 44% of Miami-Dade closings were all-cash in early 2026, and the share is higher still in the trophy segment.
