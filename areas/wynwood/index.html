Wynwood Real Estate

Arts-district energy and new development — Miami's cultural hub, now one of its most talked-about places to live and invest.

Wynwood transformed from a low-rise arts district into a dense, mixed-use neighborhood where galleries, murals, restaurants, offices, and new residential towers all coexist — one of Miami's most distinctive places to live and invest.

Why invest in Wynwood?

A 2015 rezoning unlocked taller, higher-density development, and the neighborhood has been building ever since. New construction is priced as lifestyle luxury — roughly $850 to $1,100 per square foot — and many projects are purpose-built for short-term-rental flexibility, including DUOS Wynwood and a PMG/LNDMRK tower of fully furnished studios and one-bedrooms that secured $126 million in financing.

The fundamentals behind the demand are real: Wynwood's population has grown from about 1,000 before rezoning to roughly 6,000, with thousands more residents on the way, and major employers like Amazon and Spotify have arrived. For investors, that means strong short-term-rental potential and a young, affluent renter base.

What is it like to live in Wynwood?

Wynwood is walkable, creative, and social — street art, galleries, breweries, chef-driven restaurants, and nightlife at the doorstep. It suits younger buyers, creatives, and investors who want cultural cachet and rental upside over a traditional residential feel.

Questions, answered

Wynwood real estate FAQ

Is Wynwood a good real estate investment?

Wynwood pairs a strong cultural brand with fast growth and a wave of short-term-rental-friendly new construction, giving investors access to a young, affluent renter base. Confirm each building's rental rules and run the numbers on a specific unit before buying.

What is the price per square foot in Wynwood?

New-construction condos generally run $850 to $1,100 per square foot, priced as lifestyle luxury.

Why is Wynwood growing so fast?

A 2015 rezoning allowed taller, denser development, and the arrival of employers like Amazon and Spotify plus new dining and hotels has driven the population from about 1,000 to roughly 6,000, with more coming.

Is Wynwood good for short-term rentals?

Several new buildings are purpose-built for short-term-rental flexibility, and tourist demand is strong — making well-chosen units attractive for STR investors who confirm building policies first.
