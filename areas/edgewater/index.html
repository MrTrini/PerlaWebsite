Edgewater Real Estate

Waterfront towers on Biscayne Bay — luxury pricing without Miami Beach entry points, minutes from Wynwood and the Design District.

Edgewater runs along Biscayne Bay just north of Downtown, and it has become one of Miami's fastest-rising luxury neighborhoods — a wall of new waterfront towers with bay views, private marinas, and resort-level amenities.

Why invest in Edgewater?

Edgewater shows luxury pricing signals without the friction of Miami Beach entry points — median asking prices around $699,000 and roughly $639 per square foot as of late 2025. Urban, walkable districts like this tend to benefit early as the market normalizes, and demand keeps arriving with each new delivery.

The waterfront pipeline is deep: towers like Missoni Baia, Elysee, Paraiso Bay, and Biscayne Beach anchor the skyline, and newer projects such as The Cove Residences — which broke ground in 2026 with pricing from about $795,000 to $10 million — continue to raise the ceiling. For investors, well-positioned units perform strongly in the short-term rental market thanks to the neighborhood's walkability and bay access.

What is it like to live in Edgewater?

Edgewater stretches from Biscayne Bay to NE 2nd Avenue, placing residents within minutes of Wynwood, Midtown, the Design District, Downtown, and Miami Beach. It's a bayfront, high-rise lifestyle — waterfront parks, new dining, and skyline-and-water views — with more room to buy than the barrier islands.

Questions, answered

Edgewater real estate FAQ

Why is Edgewater one of Miami's fastest-growing neighborhoods?

Edgewater offers genuine Biscayne Bay waterfront and a walkable, central location next to Wynwood, Midtown, and the Design District — at pricing below the barrier islands. A steady pipeline of luxury towers keeps drawing residents and investors.

What is the average price in Edgewater?

As of late 2025, median asking prices were around $699,000, or roughly $639 per square foot, with new luxury towers ranging well into the millions.

Is Edgewater good for short-term rental investment?

Its walkability, bay access, and proximity to Wynwood and Miami Beach make well-located units attractive for short-term rentals. Confirm each building's rental rules before buying — policies vary.

How close is Edgewater to the rest of Miami?

It's minutes from Downtown, Wynwood, Midtown, the Design District, and a short drive to Miami Beach.
