Frida Kahlo Wynwood Residences

The world's first Frida Kahlo–branded residences — art-forward living in the Wynwood Arts District.

Frida Kahlo Wynwood Residences is the world's first Frida Kahlo–branded residential development — officially licensed by the Frida Kahlo Corporation — in the heart of the Wynwood Arts District at 119 NW 29th Street.

The opportunity

Co-developed by PMG and LNDMRK Development, designed by internationally known architect Carlos Ott with Cube 3, and with interiors by Cotofana Designs. Two towers of 8 and 14 stories hold 244 residences, with a curated Frida Kahlo art program running through the property, including a large Kahlo mural on the taller tower.

Studios through three-bedrooms and penthouses run from about 400 to 1,170 square feet, delivered fully furnished — most with a dedicated furnished home-office suite — and short-term rentals are permitted. Amenities include an elevated resort-style pool, a thermal-circuit wellness center with sauna, steam and cold plunge, an outdoor bar, and an on-site Baker Health concierge-medicine center. Delivery is targeted for 2028.

Questions, answered

Frida Kahlo Wynwood Residences FAQ

Where is Frida Kahlo Wynwood Residences located?

At 119 NW 29th Street in Miami's Wynwood Arts District — steps from the galleries and minutes from the Design District and Downtown.

What are prices at Frida Kahlo Wynwood Residences?

Roughly $500,000 to $1.6M for furnished studios through three-bedrooms (2026 figures, subject to change). Ask me for current availability.

Is it officially licensed by Frida Kahlo?

Yes — it is officially brand-licensed by the Frida Kahlo Corporation, which curates the art program, food and beverage, and pool deck.

Can I rent my unit short-term?

Yes — short-term rentals are permitted, and most homes include a furnished office suite, making it investor-friendly.

When will it be completed?

Sales launched in 2026 and the project is pre-construction, with delivery targeted for around 2028.
